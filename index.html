LAND’S END TO JOHN O’GROATS 2026

A Solo & Unsupported Journey by Nick O’Connell

In Support of the Alzheimer’s Society

THE STORY

In April 2026, Nick O’Connell set out from the southwestern tip of England to tackle the legendary "End to End." Riding solo and unsupported, he carried everything he needed to cover 1,000 miles and 14,000 meters of climbing.

Inspired by the memory of two family members lost to dementia, Nick rode to raise funds for the Alzheimer’s Society. Every pedal stroke was a tribute; every mile was a step toward a cure.

LEG 1: THE CORNISH START

Land’s End to Plymouth: The journey began with the "Cornish Rollers"—relentless, short, sharp climbs that tested the legs immediately. Nick fueled up on local pasties and cake, navigating the mist and the rugged Atlantic coastline.

LEG 2: MOORS & GORGES

The South West & The Midlands: Sunshine broke through as Nick crossed the wild expanse of Dartmoor and tackled the famous 20% gradients of Cheddar Gorge. This leg was defined by massive 100-mile days and a high-speed crossing into the heart of England.

LEG 3: THE HALFWAY MARK

The Mersey to the Lakes: Nick hit the halfway point with a "Ferry Cross the Mersey" and a push through the flat plains of Cheshire. The scenery soon turned rugged again as the Lake District mountains appeared on the horizon.

The Liverpool skyline from the water.

The halfway smile—1,000 miles is starting to feel real.

Ambleside: The start of "The Struggle."

LEG 4: CROSSING THE BORDER

The Lake District to Falkirk: The 20% gradients of "The Struggle" led to a 50mph descent and a celebratory crossing into Scotland. Nick took a moment to appreciate the engineering of the Falkirk Wheel and the scale of the Kelpies.

LEG 5: THE HIGHLAND GAUNTLET

Falkirk to Inverness: The penultimate leg brought snow-capped peaks, freezing rain, and a nasty encounter with a 2-inch nail. Despite the puncture and the cold, the warmth of Highland hospitality and the support of fellow riders kept the wheels turning.
