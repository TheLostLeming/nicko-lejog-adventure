THE STORY

In April 2026, Nick O’Connell set out from the southwestern tip of England to tackle the legendary "End to End." Riding solo and unsupported, he covered 1,024 miles and over 14,000 meters of climbing.

Inspired by the memory of two family members lost to dementia, Nick rode to raise funds for the Alzheimer’s Society. Every pedal stroke was a tribute; every mile was a step toward a cure.

LEG 1: THE CORNISH START

Land’s End to Plymouth

The journey began with the "Cornish Rollers"—relentless, short, sharp climbs that tested the legs immediately. Nick fueled up on local staples, navigating the mist and the rugged Atlantic coastline.

LEG 5: THE HIGHLAND GAUNTLET

The penultimate leg brought snow-capped peaks and freezing rain. Despite a nasty encounter with a 2-inch nail puncture, the warmth of the Highland hospitality kept the wheels turning toward Inverness.

MISSION ACCOMPLISHED!

"11 days, 1,024 miles, and 14,200m of climbing later... I’ve finally made it to John O’Groats!"

The final 190km push from Inverness was a battle against the rain and tired legs, but seeing that iconic signpost made every mile worth it. We exceeded the target for the Alzheimer’s Society together. Time for a celebratory pint!
