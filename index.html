LAND’S END TO JOHN O’GROATS 2026

A Solo & Unsupported Journey by Nick O’Connell

In Memory of Family and In Support of the Alzheimer’s Society

THE STORY

In April 2026, Nick O’Connell set out from the southwestern tip of England to tackle the legendary "End to End." Riding solo and unsupported, he carried everything he needed to cover 1,000 miles and 14,000 meters of climbing.

LEG 1: THE CORNISH START

Land’s End to Plymouth: The journey began with the "Cornish Rollers"—relentless, short, sharp climbs that tested the legs immediately. Nick fueled up on local pasties and cake, navigating the mist and the rugged Atlantic coastline.

LEG 2: MOORS & GORGES

The South West & The Midlands: Sunshine broke through as Nick crossed the wild expanse of Dartmoor and tackled the famous 20% gradients of Cheddar Gorge. This leg was defined by massive 100-mile days and a high-speed crossing into the heart of England.

LEG 3 & 4: NORTHWARD BOUND

Nick hit the halfway point with a "Ferry Cross the Mersey" before tackling the Lake District and the 50mph descent into Scotland.
